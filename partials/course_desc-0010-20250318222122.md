**Artificial Intelligence and Plain Text: Writing Tools in an Expanded Field**

In this writing intensive course we venture beyond the perimeters of word-processing programs (e.g., Microsoft Word) and web-based emulations of them (e.g., Google Docs) to explore an expanded field of modern technologies for writing.
Since the initial release of ChatGPT in November 2022, the makers of word-processing software have scrambled to integrate AI features into their products.
The competitive motive is obvious, for genAI chatbots promise to deliver writing on demand, suitable for many purposes, within a simple and intuitive user interface.
(By contrast, the user interfaces of word-processing programs have always been excessively cluttered, making writing harder than it should be.)
Meanwhile, the use of genAI as a writing tool has caused much debate and worry in academic contexts.
In this course we survey the debate and develop our own responses to it, taking a *philosophical* and *experimental* approach.

A central question is "What is writing for?"
Our answers to that question give us a basis for evaluating different writing tools and techniques.
We experiment with pen-and-paper, word-processor, genAI, dictation, and plain text.
The last of these, plain text, reduces distraction by separating *meaning* from *appearance*.
Assessment is by class participation and a scaffolded sequence of formal and informal writing assignments, with an emphasis on writing process.

Pre-requisite: UCWR 110.

**This course counts toward fulfillment of the CAS Writing Requirement.**
