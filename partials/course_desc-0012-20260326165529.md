# ENGL 322

This course explores the poetry of Geoffrey Chaucer, one of the great innovators in English literature.
At a time when the English language was not an obvious choice for an aspiring writer of literature, Chaucer employed this vernacular language as the medium for a unique poetic voice, richly drawn fictional worlds, and an inclusive vision for literature in English.
We read Chaucer's most influential and celebrated poems, including the *Canterbury Tales*, his unfinished masterpiece.
For the *Canterbury Tales* Chaucer created a diverse cast -- women and men, poor and rich, profane and devout -- who regale one another with boundary-pushing stories.
The poetry will be read in its original language, called "Middle English": the reward is an unusually close experience of literature.
Assignments include critical commentary and translation, essays, a class presentation, and midterm and final exams.
This course fulfills the pre-1700 English major requirement.

Objectives:

- Explore the imaginative possibilities of versified literary fiction
- Gain an experience of literature from another time and place
- Develop skills for understanding a form of the English language different from the modern standard
- Refine skills of formal expository writing and literary argument
