A note collection has three parts, as follows.

# Ten notes {.unnumbered}

Each note should have the following components:

1. A unique identifier for the note. This may be a timestamp or an integer between 1 and 10.

1. A unique title, to serve as a concise meaningful way of referencing the note

1. A quotation. This is the nucleus of the note.

1. The source citation in MLA or Chicago format, as it would appear in a list of Works Cited.
   For guidance see [Citing sources](#citing-sources).
<!--
   Use Zotero to generate this, or consult the *Chicago Manual of Style* [Citation Quick Guide](https://www.chicagomanualofstyle.org/tools_citationguide/citation-guide-1.html)
-->

1. A 'point locator', if relevant, allowing the quotation to be located within the source. Usually this will be the page number on which the quotation appears.

1. Commentary.
   Here you may elaborate the significance of the quotation (why is it noteworthy?),
   situate the quotation within the argument of the source,
   identify key terms or concepts,
   identify connections with other notes in your collection,
   or enter into dialogue with the quoted statement by extending it to a new topic or raising a challenge or objection.
   The commentary should be about twice the length of the quoted passage.

1. Four or five subject terms.
   Subject terms should be nouns or noun phrases (e.g. "authorship" or "social conditions") that designate central and distinctive concepts in your note.
   Imagine that you have a collection of 1,000 notes: what search terms would you use to locate this note and others on its topic?
   In a collection of research notes some subject terms will be frequent while others may appear just once, identifying a unique note on a specialized topic.

# An overview of your note collection {#collection-overview .unnumbered}

What questions organized your research?
Which sources and search-procedures were most useful and why?
Describe the movement of your attention through your sources, your centers of focus, and connections you observe between individual notes.
What questions do you have at the end of this process?
This reflective overview should be about 500 words.

# A list of subject terms in your collection {.unnumbered}

For each subject term, state the frequency of the term in your note collection (that is, the number of note-items to which the subject term is assigned).
You may organize this as a table.
